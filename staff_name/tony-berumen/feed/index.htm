The students, chaperoned by Jeff Darr, who is in charge of DECA, attended a competition focusing on innovation. In all, 32 kids went.

The DECA competition of innovation included schools from all over the United States, such as schools from Georgia and Florida. Northwest did not place this year. The schools had to do an innovative project centered around pencils. They gave pencils a completely new task and idea. Some students made bridges, some heating pads, and some pencils that recorded everything written down.

Besides the competition, the students also explored the city of Chicago. The went shopping and eating at Magnificent Mile, a ten-story tall mall, went to Shed Aquarium, and the Willis Tower.

Sophomore Connor Bobb is a student who went on the trip. He said they enjoyed their stay at the Holiday Inn. “My favorite part of the trip was probably bonding and making new friends,” Bobb said.

The drive will begin Oct. 7 and last till the 25. Brian Latta, who is in charge of Grizzlies For Change, will run the drive.

Everything collected in the event will go Goodwill. For every pound of clothes collected, the school will receive 10 cents. If the school gets up to 1000 pounds, then we will enter a drawing for $1000.

“This is the first time the school has ever done it,” Latta said.

Grizzlies For Change is also in charge of other clothes drives around the year. The two clothing bins outside on the 13th St. side of the school are managed by the organization. They get picked up yearly by Goodwill as well.

The purpose of the contest was to print, interview, write stories, take photos, and then design a four-page newsletter.

 The competition started at 8 a.m. and lasted all day until 6 p.m., which was the final deadline. Judging took place over the weekend. The schools will be contacted with the results.

The students who went were sophomores Haley Cooper and Emily Dulohery and seniors Lyndi McIntyre, Kira Crafton and Grace Buckingham.

“Even if we don’t place, I’m still glad we went,” says Morse, “it was a nice way to get out of the school setting and report on something different, and show our true skills.”

It was a fairly nice day, no pun intended. The weather was in the mid-70’s. The day started out really slow and got more busy as the day went on.

Sophomore Haley Cooper covered and wrote about the 4H Horse Show.

“It was a good experience,” Cooper said. “It was a very real world application of reporting. I feel like we did a good job.”
