Andrew Minter – Northwest Explorer https://www.northwestexplorer.org The Student Voice of Wichita Northwest High School Thu, 13 Sep 2018 20:03:12 +0000 en-US hourly 1 https://wordpress.org/?v=6.3.1 The final countdown https://www.northwestexplorer.org/news/2014/01/31/the-final-countdown/ https://www.northwestexplorer.org/news/2014/01/31/the-final-countdown/#respond Fri, 31 Jan 2014 17:56:35 +0000 http://www.northwestexplorer.org/?p=2648 The finals policy might change next year for Northwest students. Students of all grades and class levels may be required to take finals.

“Our district is planning on college career readiness,” principal Gil Alvarez said, “but no decisions are certain.” If finals are required the difficulty won’t change.

“Many schools in USD 259 are already requiring their students to take all their finals,” Alvarez said. “As far as I know all students will have to take finals in college.”

The teachers also have some input. “The teaching staff has been asked to take a survey regarding finals policy,” said history teacher Dennis Cox. “It’s a disservice to the students not to prepare them.”

Once in college the students will have to take finals and in some cases midterms.

“Finals week tends to be much more stressful to students who haven’t taken them before,” Cox said.

The students of Northwest are very opinionated on the subject.

“I wouldn’t take any hard classes,” said sophomore Lien Vu.

Some are irritated. “I’d be pretty mad,” said freshman Kelton Simkins.

The decision isn’t final, and they’re still debating the matter. For now the students will just continue the same procedures they have always done.

]]> https://www.northwestexplorer.org/news/2014/01/31/the-final-countdown/feed/ 0 Spiders, spiders everywhere https://www.northwestexplorer.org/news/2013/10/24/spiders-spiders-everywhere/ https://www.northwestexplorer.org/news/2013/10/24/spiders-spiders-everywhere/#respond Thu, 24 Oct 2013 17:37:48 +0000 http://www.northwestexplorer.org/?p=2459 As winter approaches, the world outside changes, trees lose all their leaves, families prepare for the holidays and spiders find residence in your basement.  Yes, spiders, mostly the brown recluse and the notorious black widow.

When the weather gets cold and rainy these spiders will often move inside to avoid the conditions outside.

Here is some information about each of these types of spiders:

Brown Recluse

Lives in the southeastern United States

The bite contains a hemotoxin, a poison that makes the skin around the bite corrode away.

The Brown Recluse usually only bites when pressed up against the skin, so many people don’t get bitten even though they live in houses containing them.

They live in damp and dark undisturbed areas, such as basements or closets.

 Black Widow

Named this because female has been known to kill the male after mating.

Easily identified because of its black tint with a red hourglass shape on its abdomen.

The black widow creates a messy, unorganized web, usually found in the same conditions as the brown recluse.

The bite can cause death, but only if it’s not treated within a few hours using an anti-venom.
