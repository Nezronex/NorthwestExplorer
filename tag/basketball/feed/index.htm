March 5. The team lost to Garden City in a 66-40 game, resulting in them being eliminated from qualifying for state.

“At the beginning we were working really hard and doing well holding them, but they went on a run of points and we couldn’t stop them,” said sophomore Haley Cooper. At halftime the score 29-12 Garden City.

“We went from a run of 8-0 at the beginning of the game to that, it was a little disappointing,” said Cooper.

The game was played in Garden City, so the team had to stay after the loss to watch the Northwest boys play, who also had a tragic 3 point loss.

“We were trying to be supportive of the guys during their game but when we got back into the locker room it was very emotional since it was the seniors last game,” said Cooper.

The team finished with a 9-12 record.

“Ultimately it would have been nice to have a happier ending, but it was a good experience, despite it all we functioned as a team unit and were supportive the whole season,” said Cooper.

With the opening of a new season, however, and the opening of North’s new gym, the Grizzlies ended up with a new result against the Redskins.

On Friday, Dec. 6, the boys were triumphant over North with a final score of 50-45.

Junior Aaron Nicholson was Northwest’s leading scorer with 14 points, followed by junior Nick Williams, who scored 12 points.

This victory was followed by another against West, with a final score of 62-49 on Tuesday, Dec 10.

Junior Cortlin Standifer led the team in scoring with 14 points, along with senior Kaleb Cobb who scored 11 points and senior Cortez Carr with 10.

The Grizzlies then faced a devastating loss to South on Tuesday, Dec. 17. The competitive game concluded with a score of 47-49.

Junior Nick Williams finished with 11 points and senior Brett Johnston scored 10.

The boys’ next opponent was East. The Grizzlies fell short on Thursday, Dec. 19 with a loss of 40-53.  Senior Cortez Carr finished with 15 points and sophomore Tyler Caldwell had 7 points.

Northwest then played in Koch Arena, where they procured a victory with a score of 67-53. On that Thursday, Jan. 2, senior Kaleb Cobb led the team in scoring with an astonishing 21 points, followed by Junior Nick Williams with 19.

Their most recent game was on Tuesday, Jan. 7 at Heights. They suffered a loss of 70-34.

The varsity boys play Southeast tonight at 7:30 in the Northwest gym.
