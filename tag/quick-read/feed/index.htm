Some cost and others are free. 

Spotify is a music streaming service with over 180 million active users, over 4 million songs and over 2 million artists. Spotify is free, but you can pay for Spotify Premium monthly. Spotify Premium includes ad-free music, on-demand playback, unlimited skips, and allows you to listen anywhere, even offline. For one account it’s $9.99 a month, for two accounts it’s $12.99 a month, for up to six accounts it’s $14.99 a month, and for students it’s $4.99 a month. There are extra add on for people who pay for the family plan which is Spotify kids, blocking explicit music, and family mix playlist. 

YouTube is another free music streaming app, but you can pay extra for premium. YouTube Premium is $9.99 a month after a one month free trial. YouTube Premium includes no ads and allows you to listen without Wi-Fi. 

Apple music is a streaming app for Apple products. Apple music has over 70 million songs and over 56 million users. Apple music lets you stream ad free music anywhere. Apple music is not free, but there are different monthly plans. For students, Apple music cost $4.99 a month, an individual plan is $9.99 a month after your 3 month free trial, and a family plan is $15 a month. 

Soundcloud is a free music streaming app with over 76 million monthly users, over 200 million tracks, and over 20 million creators who share their music. Soundcloud offers two Soundcloud Pro plans. They offer a monthly and a yearly plan. SoundCloud Pro cost $6 a month or $55 yearly. 

Spotify seems like the best streaming service out of these. Spotify is really good with having different genres and songs. Spotify Premium also makes the service better.  

]]> https://www.northwestexplorer.org/opinion/2021/01/11/music-streaming/feed/ 0 Supplies scarce in stores during Covid-19 https://www.northwestexplorer.org/opinion/2020/11/20/stores-during-covid-19/ https://www.northwestexplorer.org/opinion/2020/11/20/stores-during-covid-19/#respond Fri, 20 Nov 2020 19:00:32 +0000 https://www.northwestexplorer.org/?p=7842 Covid-19 cases are on the rise once again. Things that people need to fulfill their basic needs are flying off the shelves. People are starting stock up in fear of another shut down.

Limits are slowly starting to be put into to the works. Limits are starting to be put on toilet paper and paper towels where you can only buy one of each. Things like beef, pork, and chicken are starting to have limits as well. Slowly soda is starting to get limits at Dillon’s and Sam’s. 

Toilet paper is flying off the shelves like it did earlier in the year. Stores like Walmart, Dillon’s, and Costco are stocked with plenty of toilet paper in variety of brands. Sam’s on the other hand are struggling to keep toilet paper on the shelves. Once toilet paper is put on the shelves it’s gone within minuets. 

Canned food like beans, fruits, vegetables, and soup are almost impossible to find on a daily basis. Throughout the week it gets harder to find can food. It’s better to go get canned food on days a store stocks so you have a better selection before things start to disappear. 

Believe it or not soda is getting harder to find during the pandemic. Popular brands like Pepsi, Coke, and Sprite are getting harder to find at stores like Costco and Sam’s. Stores like Walmart and Dillon’s are stocked on the popular brands but non popular brands like lemonade are almost impossible to get your hands on.

The lack of materials to fulfill basic needs are one of the many problems that Covid-19 has caused.
