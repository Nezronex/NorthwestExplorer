28 Northwest students placed in the top 10 in their events.

“The students competed in one of three different areas,” said business teacher Jeff Darr. These areas include role play, written tests, and presenting their projects that were being worked on throughout the year.

Juniors Marshall Johnson was elected as Kansas DECA reporter.

“I’ve done better this year,” said Johnson. “You get to up your skills in the business world.” Johnson placed third in Entrepreneurship Innovation Plan.

Marissa Haugen placed 10th in Apparel and Accessories, second in Creative Marketing Project, and was elected as Central Region Vice-President.

“I didn’t have any free time this time, so it was really hectic, and kinda scary, and nerve wracking,” said Haugen.

The next thing students will start to prepare for is the International DECA Career Development Conference in Atlanta, Georgia on May 3-7.

“It’s not the regular ‘nerd program’,” said Johnson, “it’s more fun, people are laid back, they’re kids like you are. You get to meet new people, it’s cool.”

]]> https://www.northwestexplorer.org/news/2014/04/01/deca-competition/feed/ 0 Chicago DECA trip https://www.northwestexplorer.org/news/2013/11/22/chicago-deca-trip/ https://www.northwestexplorer.org/news/2013/11/22/chicago-deca-trip/#respond Fri, 22 Nov 2013 18:05:26 +0000 http://www.northwestexplorer.org/?p=2546 From Nov. 7 to Nov. 11, Northwest’s DECA chapter went on a trip to Chicago. The students, chaperoned by Jeff Darr, who is in charge of DECA, attended a competition focusing on innovation. In all, 32 kids went.

The DECA competition of innovation included schools from all over the United States, such as schools from Georgia and Florida. Northwest did not place this year. The schools had to do an innovative project centered around pencils. They gave pencils a completely new task and idea. Some students made bridges, some heating pads, and some pencils that recorded everything written down.

Besides the competition, the students also explored the city of Chicago. The went shopping and eating at Magnificent Mile, a ten-story tall mall, went to Shed Aquarium, and the Willis Tower.

Sophomore Connor Bobb is a student who went on the trip. He said they enjoyed their stay at the Holiday Inn. “My favorite part of the trip was probably bonding and making new friends,” Bobb said.
